## üìù Project Description

This project explores how major FMCG brands are analysed, evaluated, and strategically managed behind the scenes. Working with a large dataset of product information, pack sizes, volumes, COGS, and net sales, I built a full business case by calculating KPIs, visualizing insights, and applying commercial logic to identify growth opportunities.

The progression follows the three pillars of the case-study structure:

## 1Ô∏è‚É£ Fast-Moving Consumer Goods Analysis

In the first stage, I worked as a **Category Manager** for Healthmax and performed a full category review of the shampoo market.

### **What I did**
- Cleaned and structured a large internal dataset  
- Calculated core FMCG KPIs:
  - Market share  
  - YTD (Year-to-Date) performance  
  - MAT (Moving Annual Total)  
  - Category value & volume growth  
- Created PivotTables to analyse performance by:
  - Brand  
  - Pack size  
  - Subcategory  
  - Year & month  
- Built visuals to identify:
  - Volume drivers  
  - Category shifts  
  - Brand performance gaps

This created the foundation for understanding Healthmax‚Äôs strengths and weaknesses within the category.

## 2Ô∏è‚É£ Net Revenue Management (NRM)  
**Focus Areas: Brand Portfolio Pricing & Mix Management**

In this part, I applied NRM principles to understand how pricing, assortment, and profitability influence net revenue.

### **What I did**
- Calculated:
  - Net price (Net Sales √∑ Volume)  
  - Gross profit per unit

  - Net sales contribution per SKU  
- Built a **Price Ladder** to evaluate pack-price architecture  
- Identified pricing gaps and opportunities using Portfolio Pricing frameworks  
- Modelled the launch of a new SKU using Mix Management:
  - Expected volume  
  - Incremental category growth  
  - Margin contribution  
  - Price-positioning logic  

This allowed me to determine where Healthmax could profitably expand and optimize its offering.

## 3Ô∏è‚É£ Optimizing Net Revenue
